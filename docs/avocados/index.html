The explosion of “green gold”

How avocado production boomed in Mexico and California in the last few years, setting the stage for precarious environmental conditions in the tropical West.

By Paroma Soni

Published June 28, 2021

Some people have called avocados the new “blood diamonds” – they require a lot of water to be grown in climates that are otherwise dry and hot, like most of California and increasingly more of Mexico, which is suffering dramatic climate consequences because of clearing the land for avocado harvests. Not only do avocados lead a massive water footprint, specifically in the state of Michoacan in Mexico, they are subject to regulations by drug cartels.
